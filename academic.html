Academic

A concise academic overview of my work in classical Jingfang (经方) medicine — emphasizing the system, its textual foundation, and structured knowledge transmission.

1) Academic Orientation: Jingfang as a Clinical System

My academic work is centered on Jingfang (经方) medicine as a coherent clinical system. Rather than approaching traditional Chinese medicine as a collection of techniques, I emphasize the internal structure of classical formulas, pattern differentiation, and long-term clinical verification.

Jingfang, as I understand and practice it, represents a systematic medical logic rooted in classical texts, refined through generations of clinical application, and capable of being clearly taught and transmitted.

2) Textual Lineage and Methodological Foundation

The core textual foundation of my academic work is the Treatise on Cold Damage (Shanghan Lun), with a particular focus on formula–pattern correspondence and six-channel differentiation.

My work builds upon the clinical and scholarly lineage of my father, Dr. Lou Shaokun, whose decades of research and practice established a rigorous approach to Jingfang that integrates classical interpretation with real-world clinical outcomes.

Within this framework, formulas are not treated as isolated prescriptions, but as dynamic responses to specific physiological patterns — enabling consistent reasoning and replicable clinical decision-making.

3) Systematization and Knowledge Transmission

A central aspect of my academic work is the systematization of Jingfang knowledge for effective transmission. Through long-term clinical practice and teaching experience, I have developed a structured learning pathway that guides students from foundational understanding of classical texts to advanced clinical application of formulas and pattern differentiation.

This approach is designed to support learners at different stages — providing beginners with clear orientation and structure, while helping experienced clinicians deepen reasoning, refine pattern recognition, and strengthen clinical precision.

4) Teaching and International Academic Exchange

Based on this systematic approach to Jingfang medicine, I participate in academic teaching and professional exchange with practitioners and students in multiple regions, including Europe and North America. These activities focus on clinical reasoning, formula–pattern analysis, and the practical application of classical medical texts in contemporary clinical contexts.
